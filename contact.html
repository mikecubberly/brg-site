What to Include in Your Inquiry

To get a useful first response, include the following in your first email:

  • Company name and website. So Mike can research your ICP and fit before the call.
  • Stage and approximate revenue band. Seed, Series A, $1M ARR, $10M ARR, etc.
  • Current outbound motion (if any). In-house SDRs, existing agency, or starting from zero.
  • What you think your ICP is. Short paragraph. Not required to be perfect.
  • Engagement tier of interest. DFY, IOY, or Lite. If unsure, say so.
  • Timeline. Urgent, next quarter, or exploring options.

Engagement Types

DFY

Done-For-You

Fully managed LinkedIn outbound on BRG sender profiles. Three-to-six month minimum. Best fit for founders with zero operational overhead.

IOY

Infrastructure-On-You

BRG orchestrates on your LinkedIn sender profiles. Six-month minimum. Best fit for teams with warm founder or VP profiles.

Lite

Lite Advisory

Coaching and frameworks for teams running their own outbound stack. Six-month minimum. ICP workshop, messaging, and ongoing coaching.

What Happens After You Reach Out

  1. Initial Email Reply (within 2 business days)

    Mike reads the inquiry and responds directly. Either schedules an intro call or, if BRG is not a fit, refers you to someone better suited.

  2. Intro Call (30 minutes)

    Covers your business, ICP, current motion, and whether BRG is a fit. No pitch deck. Conversational.

  3. Scoped Proposal

    If there is mutual fit, BRG sends a scoped proposal within five business days covering engagement type, deliverables, pricing, and timeline.

  4. Kickoff

    After contract signature, kickoff happens within one week. First ICP workshop and sender warmup (if IOY) begin immediately.
